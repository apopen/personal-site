You are growing and you need more engineers.
You hire five more and wait for output to jump.
It does not increase five times. It might not increase at all.

Here is why, and what to do instead.

## The Problem

Adding people adds complexity:

- More communication paths
- More context to share
- More process needed
- More management overhead

At some point, more people = slower. Not faster.

## What Actually Scales

**Documentation** - If knowledge lives in heads, new people can't contribute. Write it down.

**Clear processes** - How do you deploy? How do you decide what to build? Make it explicit.

**Autonomous teams** - Small teams with clear ownership make decisions fast. No committees.

**Strong onboarding** - Get new people contributing quickly. Reduce the ramp time.

## The Phases

```
Phase 1 (1-10): Everyone knows everything
Phase 2 (10-50): Teams form, boundaries emerge
Phase 3 (50-200): Process, documentation, platform
Phase 4 (200+): Organization design matters
```

Each phase requires different skills. What worked before won't work now.

## The Real Lesson

Growth is about capability, not headcount. You can have 10 people who outperform 50.

Focus on what makes everyone more effective. More people is the last resort, not the first.
