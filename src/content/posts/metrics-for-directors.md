As a manager, you track team velocity. As a director, you're tracking something different.

What you measure becomes what gets optimized. Pick the wrong metrics and you'll optimize for the wrong things.

## The Wrong Metrics

**Individual productivity** - Leads to hero culture, knowledge silos, and people hiding problems.

**Lines of code** - More code isn't better. Usually it's worse.

**Number of features** - Features shipped that nobody uses count for zero.

**Utilization** - 100% utilization means no time for improvements. Technical debt accumulates. Eventually everything slows down.

## The Right Metrics

**DORA Metrics**
- Deployment frequency
- Lead time for changes
- Change failure rate
- Mean time to recovery

These correlate with high-performing teams. Every company should track them.

**Outcome Metrics**
- User satisfaction
- Business impact
- Goal completion

These connect engineering work to business results.

**Health Metrics**
- Team retention
- Engineering happiness
- Bug rates over time
- Technical debt status

These catch problems before they become crises.

## The Honest Truth

What you measure shapes behavior. Measure velocity, teams will inflate estimates. Measure lines of code, they'll write verbose solutions. Measure features, they'll ship junk.

Measure outcomes. Measure health. Measure what actually matters.

Your teams are watching what you optimize for. Make it count.
