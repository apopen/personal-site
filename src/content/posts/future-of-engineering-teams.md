The job of a software engineer in 2030 will look nothing like it does today. Here's what I think changes.

## AI Won't Replace Engineers

But it will replace a lot of what engineers do today.

Writing boilerplate. Generating tests. Refactoring for style. These become trivial. The value shifts to:

- Understanding what to build
- Making architectural decisions
- Verifying AI output
- Solving novel problems

## Remote Work Is Permanent

The debate is over. Remote is here to stay. But the challenges evolve:

- Culture building without physical presence
- Onboarding new engineers
- Avoiding two-tier employee systems (office vs remote)
- Communication across time zones

Companies that figure this out have an advantage. Those that don't will struggle.

## The Skills That Matter

Technical skills are table stakes. What differentiates:

**Communication** - Explaining complex things simply. Working across teams. Writing clearly.

**Systems thinking** - Understanding how parts interact. Seeing the whole, not just components.

**Business context** - Knowing why you're building what you're building. Connecting tech to outcomes.

**Learning ability** - Technologies change. Frameworks die. The ability to learn new things matters more than knowing specific things.

## The Honest Prediction

The title "software engineer" will become less meaningful. Everyone will use technology. The differentiators will be domain knowledge, creativity, and judgment.

Build those. They're the only skills that compound over a career.
