PHP gets no respect. Every year someone declares it dead. Yet WordPress runs 40% of the web. Laravel keeps growing. PHP 8.x is actually good.

Here's why I still reach for it.

## The Bad Old Days

Remember PHP 4? I do. It was terrible. No types. Inconsistent functions. A language held together with duct tape.

Those days are gone.

## What Changed

**Modern Syntax** - Attributes, named arguments, match expressions, union types. PHP 8.x feels like a different language.

**Performance** - JIT compilation, preloading, OPcache. The benchmarks don't lie. It's fast now.

**Ecosystem** - Composer is excellent. Laravel is brilliant. Symfony components power half the industry.

**Simplicity** - You can build something real in hours, not days. Not everything needs Kubernetes.

## When to Use It

- Web applications of all sizes
- APIs that need to ship fast
- Content management systems
- Prototypes and MVPs
- Teams that need to move quickly

## When Not to Use It

- Real-time systems (look elsewhere)
- Heavy computation (not the language's strength)
- Teams that prefer other tools (nothing wrong with that)

## The Honest Take

PHP isn't trendy. It won't make you cool at parties. But it's practical, proven, and productive.

Sometimes practical is what you need.
