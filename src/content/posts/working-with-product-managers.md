The eternal tension: PMs want features. Engineers want working code. Something has to give.

After years in rooms with both sides, here's what actually helps.

## The Translation Layer

**PMs speak in business outcomes** - "We need to increase conversion."
**Engineers speak in technical effort** - "That requires a database migration."

These are different languages. Learn both.

## Rules of Engagement

**Define "done" together** - Before building, agree on what success looks like. Same page, same expectations.

**Say no respectfully** - "We can't build that in two weeks" is better than "that's a bad idea." Give alternatives.

**Share the "why"** - Don't just say what you're building. Explain why it matters. Context creates buy-in.

**Protect engineering health** - Scope creep kills teams. Push back on last-minute additions. Badges don't matter if the team burns out.

## What Good PMs Do

- Give clear priorities
- Explain the business context
- Make tradeoffs explicit
- Take "no" as an answer
- Celebrate technical wins, not just feature launches

## What Good Engineers Do

- Ask "why" early and often
- Give honest estimates
- Flag risks before they become fires
- Understand business constraints
- Remember: shipping matters

## The Bottom Line

Both sides want the same thing: building something that works and matters. When you remember that, everything else follows.
