You could be watching Netflix. Instead, you're debugging your side project at 11pm.

Here's why that's worth it.

## What You Learn

**End-to-end thinking** - Work gives you a slice. Side projects give you the whole pie. You make the decisions. You face the consequences. You learn from both.

**New technologies** - Try that new framework. Experiment with that database. No approval process. No meetings.

**Portfolio** - Concrete proof of what you can build. More convincing than any resume.

**Fun** - Remember why you got into engineering? Building things is the point.

## The Trap

Side projects become obligations. You feel guilty when you don't work on them. That's not the point.

**Rules for healthy side projects:**

- Work on what you enjoy
- Abandon without guilt when it stops being fun
- Don't compare your project to others'
- It's practice, not performance

## What Counts As A Side Project

- Open source contributions
- Blog writing
- Learning in public
- Teaching others
- Anything you build for the joy of it

## The Bottom Line

Your career is built on what you can do, not what you did at your job. Side projects are where you grow.

Even if nobody ever sees them.
