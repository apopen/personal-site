Your platform team builds tools. Developers don't use them. They build their own workarounds.

Sound familiar?

## The Problem

Platform teams often build for themselves. They decide what developers need. They build in isolation. They surprise people with new tools.

Developers see this as bureaucracy, not help.

## What Works

**Treat developers as customers** - Understand their pain. Watch them work. Ask what they need.

**Self-service** - If developers need to file a ticket to get anything, you're creating bottlenecks.

**Minimize friction** - Every step between "I need X" and "I have X" is friction. Reduce steps.

**Iterate** - Ship something simple. Get feedback. Improve. Don't try to build the perfect system upfront.

Start with the biggest pain. Build that first.

## Measuring Success

- Time from "I need X" to "I have X"
- How many developers use your platform
- Support tickets related to your tools
- Developer satisfaction surveys

## The Real Goal

Platform engineering exists to make developers faster. If it doesn't do that, it has no value.

Ship tools that help. Measure impact. Keep improving.
