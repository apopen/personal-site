Free lunch. Nap pods. Beer on tap. These are not what make engineering cultures great.

Here's what actually is.

## What High-Performing Cultures Have

**Psychological safety** - You can say "I don't know" without fear. You can make mistakes and learn from them.

**Clear goals** - Everyone knows what success looks like. Priorities are clear. There's alignment.

**Autonomy** - People get to decide how to solve problems. They're trusted to figure it out.

**Mastery** - There's always more to learn. The work challenges people. They grow.

**Recognition** - Good work gets noticed. Contributions get appreciated.

## What Breaks Cultures

- Blame when things go wrong
- Micromanagement
- Unclear priorities
- No recognition
- Constant firefighting

## Building It

**Hire for culture add** - Not just culture fit. People who add new perspectives.

**Model the behavior** - As a leader, show the culture you want. If you want honesty, be honest.

**Reinforce consistently** - Recognize people who demonstrate values. Address behavior that violates them.

**Accept imperfection** - Cultures evolve. They'll be messy. That's fine. Keep improving.

## The Honest Truth

Culture is what you do, not what you say. The posters on the wall don't matter. The daily behavior does.

What you reward, you get. Be intentional about what you reward.
