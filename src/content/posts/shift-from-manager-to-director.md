You finally get the promotion. More money, fancier title, bigger office. Maybe even a parking spot. Then someone pulls you aside and whispers the truth: you now manage managers. Congratulations.

## What Actually Changes

**Your Calendar** - As a manager, your calendar was full of 1:1s. As a director, it's full of meetings you didn't know existed. Strategy sessions. Cross-functional syncs. Meetings about other meetings. You'll miss 1:1s and feel guilty about it. That's normal.

**Your Feedback Loop** - Before, you saw problems directly. Now, you see them three layers deep and three weeks late. You'll learn to ask better questions and trust your instincts less. Fun combo.

**Your Success Metrics** - Your team's output was your output. Now it's about enabling other managers to enable their teams. It's indirect, it's frustrating, and it's the job.

## What Doesn't Change

**You Still Need to People** - Shocking, I know. Technical skills might fade, but the ability to connect with humans, understand their motivations, and help them grow? Still everything.

**You're Still Accountable** - Things go wrong at 2am, and guess whose phone rings. The title changed, but the responsibility didn't.

**You Still Need to Learn** - The imposter syndrome doesn't magically disappear. If anything, it gets louder.

## The Real Talk

The jump from manager to director isn't about doing more. It's about letting go. Your job is no longer to do the work. It's to remove obstacles, set direction, and build other leaders.

Oh, and get ready for more politics. That's the part nobody warns you about.
