You have a bug. It's not critical. It's just annoying. Every day you say "I'll fix it tomorrow."

A month later, it's still there.

This happens to everyone. Here's why, and how to beat it.

## The Psychology

**Ambiguity** - Features have specs. Bugs often don't. What exactly is wrong? How do you know it's fixed?

**Guilt** - Someone found a bug you wrote. Fixing it feels like admitting failure.

**Complexity** - Bugs hide in corners. Sometimes fixing one creates two more. It's scary.

**No glory** - Nobody celebrates bug fixes. Feature shipping gets the attention.

## Breaking The Cycle

**Timebox it** - "I'll spend 30 minutes on this bug." Not "I'll fix it." Small commitments work.

**Write a test first** - Tests make bugs concrete. They define "fixed." They prevent regression.

**Accept imperfection** - Bugs happen. Fixing them isn't admitting failure. It's showing you care about quality.

**Make it visible** - Put bugs on the board. Track them. Seeing them move toward done builds momentum.

## The Real Talk

Bugs don't fix themselves. They get worse. They pile up. They create technical debt.

The fastest way to fix bugs is to start. Right now. Even if it's just writing a test to reproduce it.

That's half the battle.
