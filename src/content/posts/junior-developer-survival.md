You're new. You don't know what you don't know. Everyone seems smarter, faster, more confident.

Here's what actually matters.

## The First Year

**Ask questions** - The only dumb question is the one you don't ask. Everyone expects you to need help. Ask before you spend 4 hours on something that would take 5 minutes to explain.

**Write things down** - You'll forget. Everything. Write it down. Notes, documentation, decisions. Future you will thank present you.

**Read code** - More than you write. See how others solve problems. Learn patterns. Absorb style.

**Make mistakes** - This is the time to break things safely. Learn from it. Just don't make the same mistake twice.

## What Actually Helps

- Code reviews: Your best learning tool
- 1:1s: Use them. Ask for feedback
- Side projects: Fill the gaps
- Reading: Books, blogs, docs
- Patience: Growth takes time

## What Doesn't Matter (Yet)

- Frameworks: They come and go
- Salary: Early career money is temporary
- Tech stack: Skills transfer
- Seniority: It comes with time

## The Secret

Everyone feels like an impostor. Everyone. The senior engineers too. The difference is they've learned to act anyway.

You'll get there. Give it time.
