You have a meeting.
It is an hour.
Six people attend.
That is six hours of focused work that just vanished.

Now do that five times a day.
No wonder nothing gets done.

## The Multiplication

```
Meeting cost = duration × attendees × average salary

Example:
1 hour × 6 people × $100/hr = $600

Per week: $3,000
Per month: $12,000
Per year: $144,000
```

Meetings are expensive. Most teams don't think about this.

## Meetings That Work

- Clear purpose defined upfront
- Required attendees only
- Timeboxed
- Action items at the end

## Meetings That Don't

- "Let's sync"
- No agenda
- Everyone must attend
- End without next steps

## Alternatives

- Async updates in Slack
- Written documents for decisions
- Short written responses
- Recordings for those who can't attend

## The Rules

**No standing meetings** - Recurring meetings should prove their value. Kill them if they don't.

**Cut in half** - Your meetings can probably be half as long. Try it.

**Asynchronous first** - If it can be an email, make it an email.

**Required reading** - Share context ahead of time. Don't waste meeting time on exposition.

## The Bottom Line

Every meeting should produce something. A decision. An action item. Alignment. If not, skip it.
