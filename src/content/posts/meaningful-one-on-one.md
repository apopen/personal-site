You have a 1:1 with your reports. Every week. Usually it's:
"How's it going?"
"Fine."
"Any blockers?"
"Nope."
"That's great."

This is a waste of time for everyone.

## What 1:1s Should Be

**A relationship conversation** - Not status updates. Those happen in Slack, email, standup. This is about the human.

**Forward-looking** - What's coming up? What concerns do you have? What help do you need?

**Safe space** - This is where people can be honest. Problems get surfaced before they become crises.

## Questions That Work

- What's the biggest use of your time right now?
- What's frustrating you?
- What do you wish you were working on instead?
- How can I help?
- What would make your job easier?

## What You Should Avoid

- Status updates (standup is for that)
- Only talking about problems
- Doing all the talking
- Making it about you
- Canceling when "busy"

## The Pattern

```
5 min: Their agenda
30 min: The conversation
5 min: Action items
```

Let them drive. You facilitate.

## The Payoff

Good 1:1s build trust. They surface issues early. They make people feel valued.

Bad 1:1s are just another meeting. Skip them if that's what they are.
