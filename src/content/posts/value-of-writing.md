You write code all day.
Maybe you write some docs when someone asks nicely.
That is where most engineers stop.

Here is why you should write a little more than that.

## What Writing Does

**Clarifies thinking** - Try explaining something and you realize what you don't know.

**Builds reputation** - Good writing gets noticed. People remember writers.

**Teaches others** - Your knowledge helps the team. Multiply your impact.

**Improves code** - If you can't explain it, maybe you don't understand it.

## What To Write

- Technical tutorials
- Architecture decisions
- Post-mortems
- Code reviews (yes, writing)
- Internal documentation
- Blog posts

## Getting Started

**Start small**: A paragraph is fine.
Do not aim for perfection.

**Write regularly**: Even 15 minutes a week helps.

**Do not overthink**: The first draft is always bad.
Write it anyway, then edit.

**Share it**: Writing nobody reads is practice.
Writing other people read is impact.
Share broadly.

## The Excuse

"I do not have time." You have 15 minutes. Everyone does.

"I am not a good writer." Neither was anyone else at the start.

"Nobody wants to read this." They might.
You will not know until you try.

## The Real Reason

Writing is the skill that multiplies everything else. Your code impacts your team. Your writing can impact anyone.

Start small. Keep going. Ship the words.
