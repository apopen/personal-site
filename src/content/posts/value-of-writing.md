You write code all day. Maybe you write some docs. That's it.

Here's why you should write more.

## What Writing Does

**Clarifies thinking** - Try explaining something and you realize what you don't know.

**Builds reputation** - Good writing gets noticed. People remember writers.

**Teaches others** - Your knowledge helps the team. Multiply your impact.

**Improves code** - If you can't explain it, maybe you don't understand it.

## What To Write

- Technical tutorials
- Architecture decisions
- Post-mortems
- Code reviews (yes, writing)
- Internal documentation
- Blog posts

## Getting Started

**Start small** - A paragraph is fine. Don't aim for perfection.

**Write regularly** - Even 15 minutes a week helps.

**Don't overthink** - First draft is always bad. Edit later.

**Share it** - Writing nobody reads is wasted. Share broadly.

## The Excuse

"I don't have time." You have 15 minutes. Everyone does.

"I'm not a good writer." Neither was anyone else, at first.

"Nobody wants to read this." They might. You won't know until you try.

## The Real Reason

Writing is the skill that multiplies everything else. Your code impacts your team. Your writing can impact anyone.

Start small. Keep going. Ship the words.
