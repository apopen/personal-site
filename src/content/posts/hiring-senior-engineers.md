You have five candidates. Four have perfect resumes. One has a weird background but says interesting things in the interview.

Here's how to pick.

## What Doesn't Work

**Leetcode Marathons** - Can this person reverse a binary tree on a whiteboard? Who cares. That's not the job.

**Four-Hour Onsites** - You're testing their stamina, not their engineering ability. Exhausted candidates make poor decisions.

**Resume Scoring** - Schools, companies, years of experience. These predict nothing about job performance.

## What Actually Works

**Work Sample Tests** - Give them a real problem. A day to solve it. Review the code. This correlates with actual job performance better than anything else.

**Conversation Over Quiz** - Ask about their past projects. What challenges did they face? How did they solve them? Can they explain technical decisions? This matters more than trivia.

**Reference Checks That Work** - Don't ask "would you hire again." Ask "what would they do differently" or "what was their biggest weakness." Real answers.

**Team Compatibility** - Can this person work with your team? Would people enjoy working with them? Culture add matters.

## The Simple Truth

Hire for ability to learn, not current knowledge. Hire for communication, not just technical skill. Hire people you'd want to be stuck in a problem with at 2am.

The rest is noise.
