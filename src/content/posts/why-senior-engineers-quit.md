Money keeps people in the door. It rarely keeps them there.

After years of leading engineering teams, I've watched good engineers walk. Here's what actually makes them leave.

## The Real Reasons

**No Impact** - They ship features nobody uses. Fix bugs that don't matter. Work on projects that get killed before launch. Eventually they wonder why they bother showing up.

**Bad Processes** - Excessive meetings. Pointless ceremonies. Approval chains that require seven signatures to change a variable name. Smart people will only tolerate this for so long.

**Lack of Growth** - The learning curve flattens. They've mastered the tech stack. There's nothing left to figure out. The challenge is gone.

**Toxic Culture** - Credit taken by managers. Bugs blamed on developers. Psychological safety nonexistent. No amount of stock options makes this worth it.

**Poor Leadership** - Direction changes weekly. Strategy is a mystery. Engineers are treated as resources to be allocated, not humans to be led.

## What Actually Works

Give people meaningful problems. Remove obstacles. Invest in their growth. Create space for them to do great work.

That's it. That's the whole thing.
